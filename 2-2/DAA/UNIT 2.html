Connected and Bi connected Components

Connected Components: Connected components in a graph are subsets of vertices within which every pair of vertices is connected by at least one path. In other words, a connected component is a maximal subgraph in which all vertices are connected to each other. A graph can have one or more connected components, and these components do not share any edges with vertices outside their respective component. Biconnected Components: A biconnected component is a maximal subgraph of a graph in which there are at least two distinct paths between any pair of vertices. In other words, removing any single vertex (and its incident edges) from a biconnected component should not disconnect the component. Biconnected components are important in the context of analyzing the robustness of a graph's structure and finding critical edges.
