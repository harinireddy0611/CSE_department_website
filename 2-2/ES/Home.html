ENVIRONMENTAL SCIENCE

Environmental science is a multidisciplinary field that explores the interactions between humans and the natural environment. It encompasses the study of various environmental factors, including air, water, soil, biodiversity, and ecosystems, as well as the impact of human activities on these components. Environmental scientists work to understand environmental problems such as pollution, climate change, deforestation, and loss of biodiversity, and develop solutions to address them sustainably. By combining knowledge from disciplines such as biology, chemistry, geology, physics, and social sciences, environmental science aims to promote conservation, sustainability, and the protection of natural resources for present and future generations.

Major Features of Environmental Science

  • Interdisciplinary Approach.
  • Study of Environmental Systems.
  • Environmental Problems Identification.
  • Sustainability and Conservation.
  • Environmental Monitoring and Assessment.
  • Policy and Regulation Development.
  • Environmental Education and Awareness.
  • Research and Innovation.
