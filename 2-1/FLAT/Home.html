FORMAL LANGUAGES AND AUTOMATA THEORY

FORMAL LANGUAGES AND AUTOMATA THEORY

Formal languages and automata theory is a branch of theoretical computer science that explores the mathematical models of computation. It investigates the properties of formal languages, which are sets of strings of symbols, and the automata, which are abstract machines capable of recognizing or generating these languages. This field is crucial for understanding the capabilities and limitations of computational systems, forming the theoretical basis for various aspects of computer science, including compiler design, natural language processing, and algorithm analysis.

Major Features of Formal Languages and Automata Theory

  • Formalization.
  • Language Classification.
  • Automata Models.
  • Expressive Power.
  • Decidability and Complexity.
  • Closure Properties.
  • Applications.
  • Equivalence and Minimization.

COURSE OUTCOMES

  • Design of regular expressions for language constructs and conversions of NFA to DFA.
  • Demonstrate the derivations and properties of context free grammars.
  • Analyze the applications of pushdown automata.
  • Construct DFA for Right Linear Grammar and Left Linear Grammar.
  • Appreciate the role of the Turing machine as computational and universal machine.

SYLLABUS

UNIT-I

Fundamental concepts: Strings, Alphabets, Language operations, Regular Expressions, Regular Languages: Finite automata, Types of finite automata (FA)-Non deterministic Finite Automata (NFA), Deterministic Finite Automata(DFA), NFA with ɛ-Moves, regular expression representation; Regular expressions to NFA; NFA with ɛ-Moves to NFA without ɛ-Moves; NFA to DFA Conversions; Minimization of DFA (Proofs Not Required)

UNIT-II

DFA with more than two outputs: Moore and Melay machines, Pumping Lemma for Regular Sets: Closure properties of Regular Sets (Proofs Not Required): Context Free Grammars (CFG), Right most, Left most –derivations, Parse Trees; Operator Grammar: Unit productions; Chomsky normal forms; (Proofs Not Required).

UNIT-III

Left recursion and Elimination of left recursion in CFG: Elimination of useless symbols and unit productions; Greibach Normal Form, Push Down automata (PDA): Types of PDA: Design of a PDA for a given CFG. (Proofs Not Required)

UNIT-IV

Regular Grammars (RG), Design of DFA for a given RG: Right linear and left linear Grammars and conversions: Definition of Context Sensitive Grammar (CSG) and Linear bounded automata (LBA) (Proofs Not Required).

UNIT-V

Definition of unrestricted Grammar and Turing Machine (TM): Chomsky hierarchy on Languages, Grammars and recognizers; Design of TM as recognizer; Types of TM: Computational problems of TM with multiple tracks; Decidability Problem; Churches hypothesis (Proofs Not Required)
