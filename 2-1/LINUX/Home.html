DESIGN THINKING

Design thinking is a problem-solving methodology that prioritizes human-centric solutions through a creative and iterative process. It involves empathizing with users to understand their needs, defining the problem, ideating potential solutions, prototyping and testing those solutions, and finally implementing the most effective ones. This approach encourages collaboration, experimentation, and a willingness to embrace failure as part of the learning process. Design thinking is not limited to designers; it's a mindset that can be applied across various industries and disciplines to innovate and address complex challenges effectively. By focusing on empathy, creativity, and user-centered solutions, design thinking enables organizations to develop products, services, and experiences that truly resonate with their target audience.

Major Features of Design Thinking

  • Human-Centric Approach.
  • Iterative Process.
  • Creative Ideation.
  • Prototype-driven Development.
  • Collaborative and Multidisciplinary Teams.
  • Bias Towards Action.
  • Focus on User Experience (UX).
  • Iterative Feedback Loop.
