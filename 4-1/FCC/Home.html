FUNDAMENTALS OF CLOUD COMPUTING

The fundamentals of cloud computing revolve around the delivery of computing services over the internet, providing on-demand access to a shared pool of resources such as computing power, storage, and applications. This technology enables organizations to scale resources dynamically, pay only for what they use, and benefit from flexibility and agility in deploying applications and services. Key characteristics of cloud computing include self-service provisioning, elasticity, resource pooling, and measured service. By leveraging cloud computing, businesses can reduce infrastructure costs, improve scalability, and accelerate innovation while focusing on core competencies.

Major Features of Fundamentals of Cloud Computing

  • On-Demand Self-Service.
  • Elasticity and Scalability.
  • Resource Pooling.
  • Broad Network Access.
  • Measured Service.
  • Rapid Provisioning and Deployment.
  • Fault Tolerance and High Availability.
  • Security and Compliance.
