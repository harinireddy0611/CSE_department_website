COMPUTER NETWORKS

Computer networks are intricate systems that enable computers and other devices to communicate and share resources, facilitating seamless information exchange across various locations. These networks can be as small as two interconnected devices or as vast as the internet itself. They rely on protocols and technologies to establish connections, manage data transmission, and ensure security. Computer networks play a pivotal role in modern society, powering everything from simple email exchanges to complex global transactions, and underpinning the infrastructure of the digital world.

Major Features of Computer Networks

  • Connectivity.
  • Resource Sharing.
  • Scalability.
  • Reliability.
  • Security.
  • Speed.
  • Interoperability.
  • Centralized Management.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

  • Understand OSI and TCP/IP models.
  • Understand Data Link Layer protocols.
  • Analyze various routing protocols.
  • Mention Transport layer protocols.
  • Outline Application Layer.

COURSE OUTCOMES

  • Analyze TCP/IP and OSI models and various protocols.
  • Identify suitrable multiple access for different protocols.
  • Analyze various routing protocols.
  • List techniques to improve QoS.
  • Evaluate various responsibilities of application layer.
